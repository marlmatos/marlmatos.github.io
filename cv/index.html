Work

  • 2026.03 - Present
    Functional Genomics Postdoctoral Associate
    New York Genome Center
    • Expand machine learning approaches for functional variant prioritization through evaluation and integration of complementary predictive methods.
    • Build integrative analyses combining functional genomics and CRISPR perturbation data to improve variant-to-gene mapping and causal mechanism prioritization.
  • 2019.08 - 2026.02
    Human Genetics Researcher (PhD research; in collaboration with New York Genome Center, 2022–2026)
    Albert Einstein College of Medicine
    • Led a cohort-scale human genetics study in primary CD4+ T cells, generating a pan-CD4+ T-cell multi-omic map to define regulatory mechanisms underlying immune phenotypes, age-associated variation, and disease-relevant biology.
    • Identified and prioritized thousands of regulatory variants influencing gene expression and chromatin accessibility through molecular QTL mapping, providing mechanistic context for disease-associated loci across 46 immune-mediated diseases and traits.
    • Trained and benchmarked sequence-based machine learning models of regulatory activity against empirical QTL data to predict effects of fine-mapped disease-associated variants in LD.
    • Developed scalable computational workflows for cohort-level multi-omic preprocessing, including normalization, quality control, and integrative downstream analysis.
  • 2017.08 - 2019.07
    Functional Genetics and Stem Cell Researcher
    Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai
    • Investigated molecular and cellular effects of disease-associated genetic variants using stem-cell and gene-editing approaches, generating mechanistic insights into variant function in disease-relevant contexts.
    • Standardized experimental workflows, data collection, and analysis practices to reduce technical variability and improve reproducibility across studies.
